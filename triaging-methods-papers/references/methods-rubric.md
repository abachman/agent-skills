# Methods-Building Rubric

Use this rubric for quick triage and consistency across notes.

## Dimensions

Score each dimension as `High`, `Medium`, or `Low`.

1. Method transferability
   - High: design/measure/analysis is reusable in multiple future projects
   - Medium: partially reusable with adaptation
   - Low: idiosyncratic or hard to reapply

2. Internal validity quality
   - High: clear design choices and confounds addressed
   - Medium: some validity discussion, notable gaps
   - Low: weak controls or unclear causal logic

3. Measurement quality
   - High: constructs are clearly operationalized; reliability/validity reported
   - Medium: partial reporting; some ambiguity
   - Low: poorly specified or ad hoc measures

4. Replicability signals
   - High: protocol, instruments, code, or data are available or well documented
   - Medium: enough detail to approximate replication
   - Low: key implementation details missing

5. Context portability
   - High: likely to transfer across settings/populations
   - Medium: transfer possible with constraints
   - Low: very context-bound

## Triage Decision

- `Read deeply`
  - Mostly High/Medium; strong transferability and adequate trustworthiness
- `Read selectively`
  - Mixed profile; one or two high-value methods components
- `Skim`
  - Limited methods reuse or low portability
- `Archive`
  - Minimal relevance for methods-building at this time

## Red Flags to Record

- Underpowered sample without caveat
- Construct-measure mismatch
- Weak or missing baseline/comparison
- Selective reporting or unclear outcome definitions
- Claims stronger than evidence supports

## Confidence Guidance

Add a confidence tag for the overall note:

- `high`: core methods details are explicit and grounded
- `medium`: some key details are inferred from context but mostly grounded
- `low`: major details are missing, ambiguous, or extraction quality is poor
